\section{Results}

Applying the logarithmic mass index to all known Standard Model particles yields integer values with deviations well below experimental uncertainties. Figure~\ref{fig:qmass} demonstrates the linear alignment of particle masses in logarithmic space.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figures/q_vs_mass.pdf}
\caption{Logarithmic mass index versus physical mass for Standard Model particles.}
\label{fig:qmass}
\end{figure}

A three-dimensional lattice embedding reveals clear geometric clustering by particle type, as shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:lattice}.
